Gentleness:
Meek - Prades
Meek and Gentle are very similar.
Matthew 5:5 "Blessed are the meek for they shall inherit the earth"
When you think of the word meek, one may think of "weak;" It may seem that
one can easily walk all over.
When in reality, there is strength in restraint.

Take the example of the wild stallion. A stallion that is untamed and rampet.
Once this stallion is tamed he becomes more util, but his power remains.

Similarly, when gentleness is used, we will be more useful; we will have more power.

Usually we think that the powerful will be the once to inherit the earth, but God says otherwise.

Proverbs 15:1 "A soft answer turns away wrath, but a harsh word stirs up anger."

We automatically respond to someone else's tone.
Concerns about dealing with siblings and others can be address with
a meek, gentle, restrained response.

1 Corinthians 4:13
"Being defamed, we entreat. We have been made as filth of thr world,
the offscouring of all things until now."

Our immediate recation to people speaking negatively of us, we often respond elevately.
In the moment when people say something, especially if you know it's not true, just RELAX.
Who do we think we are to demand respect 24 /7
"Who is man that though are mindful of us"

Titus 2:8 "Sound speech that cannot be condemned, that one who is an
opponent may be ashamed, having nothing evil to say of you."

When someone is rasing a false testimony about you to someone who knows you well, the person
listening will know that they are speaking lies.
2 Timothy 2:25 "In meekness correcting those who are in opposition,
if God perhaps will grant them repentence, so that they may know the truth"

Correction is done in meekness, not in a condemning fashion. Meekness is going to the person with Christ's love.
If someone comes to you in opposition to your belief system, don't argue with them.
