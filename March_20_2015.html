Psalm 84
 For the choir director: A psalm of the descendants
of Korah, to be accompanied by a stringed instrument.

1 How lovely is your dwelling place,

O Lord of Heaven's Armies.

2 I long, yes, I faint with longing

to enter the courts of the Lord.

With my whole being, body and soul,

I will shout joyfully to the living God.


// How often do you come to church with this kind of desire as opposed to an obligation?
// At times, our flesh can 'long for the courts of the Lord' and other days may not be so.
// Sanctuary is a place where the Lord wants to gather his people and speak to them.

3 Even the sparrow finds a home,

and the swallow builds her nest and raises her young
at a place near your altar,

O Lord of Heaven's Armies, my King and my God!
4 What joy for those who can live in your house,

always singing your praises. Interlude

5 What joy for those whose strength comes from the Lord,

who have set their minds on a pilgrimage to Jerusalem.

6 When they walk through the Valley of Weeping,

it will become a place of refreshing springs.

The autumn rains will clothe it with blessings.

7 They will continue to grow stronger,

and each of them will appear before God in Jerusalem.

8 O Lord God of Heaven's Armies, hear my prayer.

Listen, O God of Jacob. Interlude

9 O God, look with favor upon the king, our shield!

Show favor to the one you have anointed.

10 A single day in your courts

is better than a thousand anywhere else!

I would rather be a gatekeeper in the house of my God

than live the good life in the homes of the wicked.

11 For the Lord God is our sun and our shield.

He gives us grace and glory.

The Lord will withhold no good thing

from those who do what is right.

12 O Lord of Heaven's Armies,

what joy for those who trust in you.


Psalm 143
A psalm of David.

1 Hear my prayer, O Lord;

listen to my plea!

Answer me because you are faithful and righteous.


// He addressed himself to God in a way that he banks on God's righteousness, not his own.

2 Don't put your servant on trial,

for no one is innocent before you.


// NO MAN living shall be justified.


Always have to remind ourselves that anything we do, regardless of how good
it may be, it does not justify us before God. It does not make us holier or
righteous. It is what HE does for us, not what we do for him.

"But we are all as an unclean thing, and all our righteousnesses are as filthy
rags; and we all do fade as a leaf; and our iniquities, like the wind, have taken us away."
Isaiah 64:6

// One that prays and fasts a lot can easily justify themselves, but the word
shed light on such a matter.

Why does God permit trails to come into our lives?


They more you put your part into becoming a child of God, the harder things tend to become.

We must remind ourselves that we have a very real adversary. They more we want
to do what is right, the more our adversary will want for us to fall astray.

"For we wrestle not against flesh and blood, but against principalities,
against powers, against the rulers of the darkness of this world, against
spiritual wickedness in high places." Ephesians 6:12

Trials lead us to become more dependent on Him and may be a reason as to why they are
permitted.

We need to be aware that there is price to be paid in seeking Him.

1 Peter 4 // Talks about being put in the furnace. The closer we get to God, the hotter things get.

1 Peter 4:1 "So then, since Christ suffered physical pain, you must arm yourselves with the same attitude he had,
and be ready to suffer, too. For if you have suffered physically for Christ, you have finished with sin

// Arm ourselves with what? Suffering of Christ. This reminds us that there are no shortcuts.
1 Peter 4:15-17
"If you suffer, however, it must not be for murder, stealing, making trouble, or prying into other
people's affairs."
"But it is no shame to suffer for being a Christian. Praise God for the privilege
of being called by his name!"
// Suffering as a Christian entails us to be different, standing out.

"For the time has come for judgment, and it must begin with God's
household. And if judgment begins with us, what terrible fate awaits those who have never obeyed
God's Good News?"

// Why does God want judgement to start in the House of God? So we won't be judged on that day
and since we will judge the world, we will need to be righteous in our judgement.

When someone has a passion for God, you can notice right away. When one falls in
ove with someone, all you can think and talk about is that person. In the case of Christ,
when we love him, it will be evident to all because they will talk about Him, etc.

When you have a passion for God, if prayer starts at 9:30, you don't arrive at 9:30 or
10:00, prayer begins when you wake up; you are not merely playing the religious game but rather have
a true hunger for Christ

When people begin to get cold or indifferent or begin to question God during their
circumstances, they begin to start walking backwards. They begin to miss one Sunday,
then an other. Eventually they stray so far that their heart becomes divided.

What is in the heart? Whatever you hold in high regard.

1 Peter 4:18
"And also,

If the righteous are barely saved,
what will happen to godless sinners?"


// Scarcely -- this word eliminates the masses of people.
// This means that believers will need to put on an extraordinarily amount of effort.

"But he that knew not, and did commit things worthy of stripes, shall be beaten with few stripes.
For unto whomsoever much is given, of him shall be much required: and to whom men have committed much,
of him they will ask the more." Luke 12:48


Most people want to be on a level with others, but equality does not exist in
this sense. We can see from the scripture that to those who have been walking
with the Lord for a longer period, they are to live a more devoted, involved level.

When God's judgement is implored, God does not judge all equally.
For example, a teacher of God's Word is judged differently and more is expected of them.

When God gives us more, we should most certainly change.

--------- A talk about women

A woman represents the bride, an important role.

Shouldn't you be able to distinguish a believer and nonbeliever?
One may say "God looks at the heart."
Yes, he most certainly does.

When women backslide, they often first turn to friends that are not Godly and will
have noticeable exterior differences.

----------- We are immortal

"And in those days shall men seek death, and shall not find it;
and shall desire to die, and death shall flee from them.
When the Lord comes, there will be no more death." Revelation 9:6

// When the Lord comes our mortal bodies must put on immorality. There will be no such thing
as death.

----------- Redeeming the time

"Redeeming the time, because the days are evil." Ephesians 5:16

Redeeming the time means there is an exchange in activity. If you lost time doing X,Y,Z, then
that is to be replaced with activity that will draw you closer to the Lord.
When people "redeem" their time, it means that past time that was wasted must be replaced with
something different; there must be change.

Are you making an honest effort, or are you just moping around?
God can not be mocked.
Are we asking the Lord for help?
Do not measure yourself with others around you, and if you have a title of a minister, you better minister.
