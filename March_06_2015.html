Story of Moses:


He was raised as an Egyptian and if one reads between the lines, one can pick up a hint of arrogance found in his
days spent in Egypt.
Given that he was an Israelite, as a leader, he was in a position of power and wanted
to take manners into his own hands. If someone was putting your family or heritage in harms way or bringing social
oppression to your interested party and you happened to be in a position of authority, would you not also want to
take matters into your hands? One can pick up the pride that was in Moses when he put to death and Egyptians
life for harassing an Israelite.

In order to humble Moses, God had to take all of his luxuries and sent him off away from Egypt.
As a shepherd (Exodus 2) and delivering a group of women, he proved his new found humility to the Lord.
He was in a monotonous lifestyle as a shepherd. On an ordinary day, he sees the burning bush. He sees that he has
ample humility and a change in character; it was then when he appeared before Moses in the form of a burning
bush and was given instruction to deliver the nation of Israel. The Lord knew what Moses needed in order for him come
to a place of humility. After Moses arrived to that place of humility, the Lord brought to his agenda into fruition, but not
until he saw that Moses was reverent (Exodus 3 as he stands in front of the bush) and humble (tending sheep) that this
supernatural, historical, life changing event would happen.
