How to obey the word of God?
(Bro Phillip's explanation)
Jewish thought: Obey first, question later.

-- Knowledge comes from the word of God. // You won't know it, unless you read it.

1. Read the Word of God
2. Fear the Lord
3. Deny ourselves

ex. Abraham followed exact orders, immediately.
ex. When the Lord came, one of his disciples said, "Let me bury my father." The Lord said,
"Let the dead bury the dead." Matthew 8:22

Listening to the Word of God, goes against everything we think we should do.

We are all okay with revelations, but the minute someone corrects our lifestyle, offense if formed,
immediately. The flesh that wars continuously in us. Romans 7:23

It's HARD to obey God, not easy.

Daniel prayed three times a day because he was an understanding person.

What does it mean to be an understanding person?
-- Man/woman of prayer, man/woman that reads the word
-- If we don't have a tender heart and do not have an understanding, we won't be able to obey God.

Obeying the word of God is denying everything that is you and obeying the Word of God. When you
get to the point where you can deny yourself, forgetting vanity, anything that has to do with this
world, it will be apparent to others.

What does it mean to deny yourself? // we can say the right things, but until we actually do, we'll understand it.

How do we get from "I have to do it" to "I want to do it, this is who I am, this is me denying what I want and giving to others"?
-- Analyze what God has given you then give what you have been given.
-- "If you do it to the least of these, you do it to me."
Ex.
1. Doing something for someone else that needs it.
2. What will you do with bonuses?
3. Someone comes over and brings dinner that I already cooked, give it to someone else instead.

Various artists started their singing careers in the church and branched out into the world.

If we don't deny ourselves in our talents to give to others, we become arrogant.

- What good is it to die physically if you're already dead?
- What is there in this world to live for?

If he is your daily bread, if his Spirit is in my, we shouldn't have this question.

Hebrews "He hardened heart for a purpose?" // To display God's power.

If God is not your source, if he is not showing his glory in your life, then it is apparent.
